## Part 2: Lead Qualification

### Qualify Before Scheduling Estimate
Ask these 4 questions on the first call:

1. **"What type of heating/cooling do you have now?"**
   - Existing geo = repair/rebuild/replace opportunity
   - Propane or electric resistance = strongest geo ROI case
   - Natural gas = longer payback, still viable in CKenergy territory with rebate

2. **"Who's your utility company?"**
   - Determines rebate eligibility — CKenergy territory is the priority
   - CKenergy customers: confirm they're in the 10-county territory (Blaine, Caddo, Canadian, Comanche, Custer, Dewey, Grady, Kiowa, Roger Mills, Washita — NOT Kingfisher County)

3. **"Do you own your home / how long have you been there?"**
   - Renters and short-term owners rarely convert on geo
   - 10+ year homeowners are ideal (long payback period works in their favor)

4. **"What's your lot size / do you have acreage?"**
   - Determines loop field type (horizontal needs more land, vertical works on small lots)
   - Pond loop if pond or large stock tank on property — cheapest install option

### Red Flags (likely won't close)
- Renting or planning to sell within 3 years
- On natural gas with very low utility bills
- Lot too small for horizontal, unwilling to pay for vertical drilling
- Outside service area

## Part 3: The Estimate Visit

### Before You Go
- Pull utility provider confirmation (check CKenergy.coop territory map if Canadian County)
- Bring: rebate sheet for their specific utility, financing brochure, Manual J worksheet
- Have GeoElite and IGSHPA credentials visible on truck/shirt

### On-Site Assessment (30–45 min)
1. Walk the property — assess lot size, note any pond/water feature
2. Check existing mechanical room — location for unit, ductwork condition
3. Assess loop field options: horizontal, vertical, or pond
4. Review existing system condition if replacing geo (check loop pressure)
5. Check electrical panel — geo requires dedicated circuit, may need upgrade

### Manual J Load Calculation
- Required on all new geo installs — do not skip
- Documents: square footage, insulation, windows, orientation, local climate data
- Right-sizing is a selling point: "I'm the only contractor in this area who calculates the exact size your home needs."

### Building the Quote
Present **3 options** when possible:

| Tier | Description | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Good | Geo Basic system, horizontal loop if site allows | Lowest upfront, same operating efficiency |
| Better | Geo Standard system, vertical loop | Better for smaller lots, mid-range |
| Best | Geo Premium system + desuperheater (free hot water) | Highest rebate tonnage, best long-term value |

Always include on every quote:
- Equipment cost
- Loop installation
- Electrical work
- Permits (if required)
- **Utility rebate amount — shown as line item offset**
- JBW extended warranty option (10-year P&L Plus: $1,399)
- Financing options (Synchrony 0%/60mo Plan 933)

### The CKenergy Close (Canadian/Blaine County only)
*"Most contractors around here quote CKenergy at $1,000 a ton — I don't know why because the actual rate is $2,000 a ton. On a 3-ton system that's a $6,000 difference in rebate right there. I've done the paperwork enough times to know."*

### Presenting the Quote
1. Start with the monthly payment (financing), not the total price
2. Show rebate as a day-one offset: "Your net investment after the CKenergy rebate is X"
3. Compare to their current utility bill: "At your current propane cost, this pays back in Y years"
4. Mention desuperheater: "This also preheats your water for free — cuts your water heater cost 30–50%"
5. Close: "Do you want to move forward today, or do you have questions I can answer?"

## Part 5: Install & Post-Sale

### Install Day
1. Confirm arrival time morning of
2. Protect floors and work area
3. Pull permit if required (Kingfisher and surrounding cities)
4. Photo documentation: loop field, equipment, electrical, before/after
5. Walk customer through system operation before leaving
6. Register ClimateMaster warranty same day (required for GeoElite warranty)
7. Enter in HCP: system info, photos, loop type, utility provider, warranty date

### Rebate Paperwork
- Submit rebate application with customer immediately after install
- CKenergy: customer submits online at ckenergy.coop — help them do it on-site if possible
- OG&E: rebates@oge.com or 877-898-1759
- CREC/OEC: check utility website for current submission process
- Keep copy of all submitted paperwork in HCP job notes

### Ask for the Review
Verbally before leaving: *"If we did right by you on this, I'd really appreciate a Google review — it helps us get in front of more homeowners who could benefit from this same system."*
Text Google review link from HCP.

### 30-Day Follow-Up
- Call to confirm system performing as expected
- Ask: "How's the utility bill looking compared to before?"
- Ask for referral: "Do you have neighbors or family who've ever asked about geothermal?"
