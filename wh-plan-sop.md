## Why This Matters (Memorize This)

**Oklahoma water is hard — 15 to 25 grains per gallon** in most of our service area.

That mineral buildup does three things:
1. **Kills efficiency** — sediment acts as insulation on the heating element. A 1/4-inch layer increases energy cost up to 40%.
2. **Shortens life** — average water heater life in Oklahoma is 8–10 years without maintenance. With annual flushing: 12–15 years.
3. **Voids warranty documentation** — most Bradford White and GE warranties require documented annual service. Without it, the warranty claim gets denied.

**The $99 plan pays for itself the first time they avoid a $111 dispatch fee on a service call.**

**The $199 plan pays for itself if it extends the water heater life by even 6 months** on a unit that would cost $1,800–$2,500 to replace.

Never pitch the plan before you've done your job. Complete the repair or assessment first. Trust is built on competence, not sales.

After completing the work, while writing up the invoice:

*"While I was in there I went ahead and checked a few things on your water heater. Can I show you what I found?"*

### Step 3 — The Condition Report (Visual + Verbal)

Point to what you see and narrate it:

**If sediment is visible or the unit is older:**
*"See this? This is mineral buildup — Oklahoma water is really hard out here, 15 to 25 grains per gallon depending on where you are. That sediment is sitting on your heating element like insulation. It's making your unit work harder than it needs to, and it's shortening the life."*

**Anode rod:**
*"The anode rod is what protects the tank from corrosion from the inside out. When it's gone, the tank starts rusting. Most people don't know it exists until the water heater starts leaking. We check that every year."*

**T&P valve:**
*"This is your safety valve — it's supposed to release pressure if things get too hot. If it fails, that's a serious safety issue. We test it every year as part of the plan."*

**Age close:**
*"How long have you been here? [X years] — so this unit is probably [X] years old. Bradford White tanks typically go 10–12 years with maintenance. Without it, you're looking at 8. You want to know before it fails, not after."*

## Part 3: FOMO & Urgency Points

These are honest urgency points — not manufactured pressure.

1. **"The sediment doesn't get better — it gets worse."** Every day without a flush, more buildup. After a certain age, flushing can actually stir up settled sediment and cause a pressure loss problem. Best to stay ahead of it.

2. **"Dispatch fee = plan cost."** The $99 Tune-Up costs the same as the dispatch fee they already paid. Framing: *"You've already spent the money — this just makes it work for you next year too."*

3. **"Warranty documentation."** Bradford White requires documented annual service. Without it, a warranty claim can be denied. The plan provides the paper trail.

4. **"We're already here."** The first inspection is happening right now. The only question is whether it's covered by a plan or not.

5. **"Hard water doesn't wait."** Mineral buildup accelerates in summer when water temps rise and usage increases. Spring is the best time to flush — before the sediment bakes in over a hot summer.

6. **"Second unit is almost free."** If they have two water heaters (common in larger homes, geo customers with desuperheater): Tune-Up adds $49 for unit 2, 360 adds $79. Frame it as: *"The second one is covered for basically nothing."*

## Part 4: Geo Customer Add-On

Geo customers with a **desuperheater** (free hot water side-effect of geo system) have a third water heater connection point — the loop.

- Desuperheater pre-heats water before it hits the tank → extends tank life
- BUT the connection hoses and heat exchanger need annual checks
- The 360 plan covers the desuperheater inspection

Script for geo customers:
*"Your geo system has a desuperheater — that's what's been giving you that preheated water. That connection needs to be checked annually too. The 360 covers that, so you're getting your geo system's water connection and your tank both looked at in one visit."*

This is a natural upsell from the Geo Basic PMA to include the WH 360 as a bundle.
